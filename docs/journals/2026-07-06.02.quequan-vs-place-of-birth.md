# Data gotcha: `birthplace` is quê quán, not place of birth

Found 2026-07-06 while completing Phan Văn Giang (Q20026119).

## The distinction

Vietnamese official bios separate two fields that `vietnam-elections`'
`person.birthplace` conflates:

- **nơi sinh** — physical place of birth
- **quê quán** — ancestral hometown / registered place of origin (analogous to
  Chinese 籍貫)

The `birthplace` field in our candidate data holds the **quê quán**, not nơi
sinh. Confirmed on Phan Văn Giang: our data gives "Xã Hồng Quang, huyện Nam
Trực, tỉnh Nam Định" (his quê quán), while his actual place of birth is Núi
Hột village, Đồng Hỷ district, Thái Nguyên (per en.wikipedia and the Ministry
of National Defence bio). Wikidata already had `P19 = Thái Nguyên` — correct.

## Wikidata mapping

- **Do NOT** map `birthplace` → `P19` (place of birth). That would be wrong.
- `quê quán` → **P66 (ancestral home)** — "place of origin for ancestors of
  subject" — is the correct property.
- If a future data pass adds a true nơi sinh field, that maps to P19.

## Scope

This affects every person record, not just this one. The plan's core statement
set (P31/P569/P21/P27/P172/P39) does not include place of birth, so nothing
already generated is wrong — but P66/P19 must be handled carefully if birthplace
is ever pushed to Wikidata.
