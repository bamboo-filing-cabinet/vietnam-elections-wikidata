# Data gotcha: `birthplace` is quê quán, not place of birth

Found 2026-07-06 while completing Phan Văn Giang (Q20026119).

## The distinction

Vietnamese official bios separate two fields:

- **nơi sinh** — physical place of birth
- **quê quán** — ancestral hometown / registered place of origin (analogous to
  Chinese 籍貫)

The `person.birthplace` field in `vietnam-elections` holds the **quê quán**, not
nơi sinh. On Phan Văn Giang our data gives "Xã Hồng Quang, huyện Nam Trực, tỉnh
Nam Định" (quê quán). Our data has **no nơi sinh field at all**; the other
location field is `current_residence` (Thái Nguyên city), not birthplace.

## Wikidata mapping

- **Do NOT** map `birthplace` → `P19` (place of birth). It is quê quán.
- `quê quán` → **P66 (ancestral home)** — "place of origin for ancestors of
  subject" — is the correct property.
- We cannot populate P19 from our data (no nơi sinh field).

## On Phan Văn Giang's actual place of birth (unresolved)

Do not treat his nơi sinh as known. Sources conflict:

- EN Wikipedia infobox: "Born … Nam Định province"
- EN Wikipedia "Early life" prose: born "Núi Hột village, Đồng Hỷ district,
  Thái Nguyên province" — contradicts its own infobox.
- Wikidata `P19 = Thái Nguyên (Q26575)` is sourced only as **imported from
  Italian Wikipedia** (P143=Q11920) — weak, secondhand.

No primary source for nơi sinh was located. Our earlier note claimed Thái
Nguyên as his verified birthplace; that came from a WebSearch summary and was
overstated. Corrected here. Leave P19 alone — not because it is confirmed, but
because it is outside what our data can verify.

## Scope

Affects every person record. The plan's core statement set
(P31/P569/P21/P27/P172/P39) does not include place of birth, so nothing already
generated is wrong — but birthplace must map to P66, never P19.
