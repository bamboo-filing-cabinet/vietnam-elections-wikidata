# Modeling multi-term NA membership

Decided 2026-07-07, grounded in existing Wikidata practice.

## Rule: one `position held` statement per legislative term

A delegate who serves multiple National Assembly terms gets **one P39
(position held = Q10841192, member of the National Assembly of Vietnam)
statement per term** — not a single statement with multiple term qualifiers.

Each statement carries its own qualifiers:
- P2937 legislative term — the specific NA term
- P768 electoral district — the electoral unit for *that* term
- P580 / P582 start / end time — that term's span
- P1111 votes received — that election's vote count (do NOT use P1107
  proportion: it is not in P39's allowed-qualifiers constraint; there is no
  sanctioned property for vote share on P39, so percentage is not recorded on
  Wikidata — it stays in vietnam-elections data)

## Evidence

Nguyễn Xuân Phúc (Q10800073) has three such statements:

| P2937 term | P768 district | dates |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 11th NA (Q107344530) | Quảng Nam (Q33356) | 2002-07-19 → 2007-07-18 |
| 13th NA (Q10810926) | Quảng Nam (Q33356) | 2011-07-21 → 2016-07-19 |
| 14th NA (Q24959152) | Haiphong (Q72818) | 2016-07-20 → … |

He switched provinces and skipped the 12th NA — which is exactly why per-term
statements are needed: district, dates, and votes vary per term, and terms can
be non-contiguous.

## Interaction with the electoral-unit model

Each term's P768 points at the persistent electoral unit *as it existed for
that term* (see `2026-07-06.03`). A delegate re-elected from the same unit
across consecutive terms has multiple P39 statements pointing at the *same* unit
item; one who moves points at different unit items.

## Batch implication

Generate one P39 statement per (person, term) the person won, keyed on
`person_id` across cycles — consistent with the person-centric mapping in
`2026-07-04.03`. Do not merge a person's terms into a single statement.
