```
== Allow proportion (P1107) as a qualifier ==

I'd like to propose adding {{P|1107}} (proportion) to the allowed-qualifiers
constraint for {{P|39}}.

'''Rationale.''' For elected positions, {{P|1111}} (votes received) is already an
allowed qualifier, but it only records the raw vote count. The candidate's
'''share of the vote''' is a standard reported election result and is not
trivially derivable from the statement alone — it requires the constituency's
total valid votes, which is not present. {{P|1107}} (proportion), a value between
0 and 1, is the natural property for this.

'''Why it matters especially for multi-member constituencies.''' In systems where
a district elects several members (e.g. Vietnam's National Assembly, where an
electoral unit returns 2–3 deputies and candidates are ranked by vote share),
the percentage is the primary comparative metric and is published as such.
Example: Phan Văn Giang ({{Q|20026119}}) was elected in Thái Nguyên electoral
unit No. 2 (15th National Assembly) with 309,433 votes = 88.68%. Recording the
count without the share loses the more comparable figure.

'''Current state.''' {{P|1107}} is used on only a handful of {{P|39}} statements
today, precisely because it is not sanctioned; there is no other allowed
qualifier that expresses vote share on {{P|39}}.

'''Alternative considered.''' Full results can also live on the election item.
That remains useful, but a per-candidate share on the person's own {{P|39}}
statement — alongside the already-allowed {{P|1111}} — keeps the individual's
record self-contained and symmetric.

Thoughts? ~~~~
```
