# Electoral units across the 2025 province mergers

Written 2026-07-09. **Extends** `2026-07-06.03` (persistent-unit model) with
guidance for the XV → XVI transition where provinces were merged.

## The problem

The `2026-07-06.03` decision says electoral units are persistent items that
span elections with stable composition, and the 2025 administrative reform
"begins a fresh generation of unit items." But what exactly does "fresh
generation" mean when:

1. The **territory** of a unit is unchanged (same communes/wards), but
2. The **province** it belongs to changed (merged), and
3. Its **number** changed, and
4. Its **seat count** changed?

Case study: Lào Cai / Yên Bái.

## The facts

### XV (2021) — two separate provinces, 4 units total

| Province | Unit | Components | Seats |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Yên Bái | 1 | TP Yên Bái, Yên Bình, Trấn Yên, Lục Yên | 3 |
| Yên Bái | 2 | TX Nghĩa Lộ, Văn Chấn, Trạm Tấu, Mù Cang Chải, Văn Yên | 3 |
| Lào Cai | 1 | TP Lào Cai, Bát Xát, Bắc Hà, Mường Khương, Si Ma Cai | 3 |
| Lào Cai | 2 | TX Sa Pa, Bảo Thắng, Bảo Yên, Văn Bàn | 3 |

Total: 12 seats across 2 provinces.

### XVI (2026) — merged province "Tỉnh Lào Cai", 4 units

| Unit | Components (now communes/wards) | Seats | Successor of |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Yên Bái area (Âu Lâu, Yên Bình, Lục Yên, Trấn Yên...) | 3 | Yên Bái unit 1 |
| 2 | Nghĩa Lộ area (Trạm Tấu, Mù Cang Chải, Văn Chấn...) | 2 | Yên Bái unit 2 |
| 3 | Lào Cai city area (Bát Xát, Mường Khương, Bắc Hà...) | 3 | Lào Cai unit 1 |
| 4 | Sa Pa area (Bảo Thắng, Bảo Yên, Văn Bàn) | 2 | Lào Cai unit 2 |

Total: 10 seats across 1 merged province.

### Wikidata state

- Yên Bái province (Q36349): P31 = "former provinces of Vietnam",
  P576 = 12 June 2025, P7888 (merged into) = Lào Cai (Q36446).
- Lào Cai province (Q36446): same QID pre- and post-merge, P2046 area
  jumped from 6,364 km² to 13,257 km² in June 2025.
- The old districts (Sa Pa, Bảo Thắng, etc.) were abolished as
  *huyện*/*thị xã* and replaced by communes/wards — but their QIDs
  still exist as former entities.

## Analysis: is XV Lào Cai unit 2 the "same" as XVI Lào Cai unit 4?

Arguments for **same entity** (extend the persistent item):
- The territory is functionally identical — the same geographic area,
  the same population, the same polling stations.
- The components (Sa Pa, Bảo Thắng, Bảo Yên, Văn Bàn) are the same
  set of areas, just now listed at commune level instead of district level
  because the district tier was abolished.
- The unit still exists; it was not dissolved. It was re-determined under
  a new resolution, as happens every election (Law 85/2015 Art. 10).

Arguments for **different entity** (new item):
- The unit number changed (2 → 4).
- The seat count changed (3 → 2).
- The containing province changed identity — it's now "Lào Cai" that
  absorbed Yên Bái, not the old stand-alone Lào Cai.
- The numbering namespace changed: "unit 2 of Lào Cai" in XV was one of
  2 units; "unit 4 of Lào Cai" in XVI is one of 4 units in a province
  that covers twice the area.

## Decision: separate official-era items

Create a new item when an electoral unit is reconstituted under a new
province/numbering namespace, even if its territory substantially matches
an earlier unit. Geography is a major continuity signal, but for Vietnamese
National Assembly units the official identity is also tied to:

- the province whose election quota the unit belongs to;
- the unit number within that province's list of units;
- the specific National Election Council resolution that establishes the
  term's units and seat counts.

The 2025 province mergers therefore begin a fresh generation of unit items.
The older unit and the newer unit may be linked as predecessor/successor
when the territory is materially continuous, but they should not be merged
into a single Wikidata item.

This matches the observed Wikidata pattern for renamed electoral districts:
Canada's `Renfrew—Nipissing—Pembroke` and `Saint-Hyacinthe—Bagot` have
separate former/current items after name-only or near-name-only 2025
changes, and the UK `Burton` / `Burton and Uttoxeter` pair is modeled as
separate items linked by `replaces` (P1365) / `replaced by` (P1366).

### Why territory is not enough

The XVI "Lào Cai electoral unit No. 2" is not the same thing as XV
"Yên Bái electoral unit No. 2" just because it succeeds the Nghĩa Lộ-area
territory. Likewise, XVI "Lào Cai electoral unit No. 4" should not absorb
XV "Lào Cai electoral unit No. 2" into the same item. The current number is
meaningful only inside the current merged-province namespace; preserving
older units as separate items prevents ambiguity when reading XV-era and
XVI-era P39 statements.

### What changed

For each continuity pair:
- The **old item** keeps its historical official label, province, parts,
  seat count, and term-qualified statements.
- The **new item** uses the current official label, current merged province,
  current parts, and current seat count.
- Link the old and new items with `replaced by` (P1366) and `replaces`
  (P1365) when the territorial continuity is verified.
- Do not use aliases like `pre-16th term` on the new item as a substitute
  for a proper predecessor item. Such aliases can confuse distinct official
  units that share a number across different province namespaces.

### Labeling

The unit was "Đơn vị bầu cử số 2 tỉnh Lào Cai" in XV and is now
"Đơn vị bầu cử số 4 tỉnh Lào Cai" in XVI. Model those as separate labels
on separate items:

- XV item en label: `Lào Cai electoral unit No. 2`
- XV item vi label: `Đơn vị bầu cử số 2 tỉnh Lào Cai`
- XVI item en label: `Lào Cai electoral unit No. 4`
- XVI item vi label: `Đơn vị bầu cử số 4 tỉnh Lào Cai`

Descriptions should include the term span and territory, e.g.:

- XV: "electoral unit for the 15th National Assembly of Vietnam election
  (2021); comprised Sa Pa, Bảo Thắng, Bảo Yên and Văn Bàn; 3 seats"
- XVI: "electoral unit for the 16th National Assembly of Vietnam election
  (2026); comprised the Sa Pa, Bảo Thắng, Bảo Yên and Văn Bàn area in
  merged Lào Cai province; 2 seats; successor to Lào Cai electoral unit
  No. 2"

### The mirror case: Yên Bái units → Lào Cai units 1–2

The same logic applies. Yên Bái unit 1 does **not** persist as the same
item as Lào Cai unit 1 in XVI. It is a predecessor item for the new merged
province unit.

- XV item en label: `Yên Bái electoral unit No. 1`
- XVI item en label: `Lào Cai electoral unit No. 1`
- XV item P131 = Yên Bái (Q36349)
- XVI item P131 = Lào Cai (Q36446)
- XV item P1366 = XVI item, and XVI item P1365 = XV item

This avoids the misleading implication that "Lào Cai unit 1" in the
merged-province namespace is the same official unit as "Yên Bái unit 1" in
the former-province namespace.

## Revised item pattern (extending 2026-07-06.03)

For units affected by the 2025 reform:

**P131 (located in)**:
- Old item: P131 = old province QID
- New item: P131 = new/merged province QID

**P1342 (number of seats)**:
- Old item: term-specific seat count, e.g. 3 for XV
- New item: term-specific seat count, e.g. 2 for XVI

**P1365/P1366 (replaces/replaced by)**:
- Add P1366 from old item to new item when the successor relationship is
  clear.
- Add P1365 from new item to old item when the predecessor relationship is
  clear.

**P527 (has part)** — components may need re-qualification:
- Old item: use the pre-reform district/town/city components used in the
  XV source.
- New item: use post-reform communes/wards if available; otherwise use the
  former district QIDs with a description/reference explaining they denote
  the same aggregate territory.

**Description** — state the term, current official unit number, territory,
seat count, and predecessor/successor relationship where relevant.

## Scope and caveats

- This analysis covers the **Lào Cai / Yên Bái** merger. The same
  pattern should apply to other merged provinces (e.g. Hà Giang), but
  each must be verified — some mergers may have actually reshuffled
  territory between units.
- "Same territory" is verified at the district level. At the commune
  level, the 2025 reform also merged communes, so the *number* of
  sub-units changed even if the aggregate territory is the same. We
  consider this equivalent (no voter moved between electoral units).
- If a future election keeps the same province namespace, number, and
  materially stable territory, the unit may continue under the same item
  per `2026-07-06.03`. The 2025 province merger is a special boundary
  between official-era generations.
